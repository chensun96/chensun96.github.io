I am a third year Ph.D student from University of Iowa and being advised by Prof. Rishab Nithyanand. I also had the pleasure of working under the mentorship of Prof. Supreeth Shastri previously. My research centers on online privacy, with a particular emphasis on privacy measurement, identifying privacy vulnerabilities, and creating tools that simplify and improve compliance with data protection regulations.

Research Projects

  • On the Suitability of LLM-Driven Agents for Dark Pattern Audits
    We design and evaluate an LLM-driven browser agent for auditing dark patterns in multi-step CCPA data rights workflows, enabling autonomous interaction, structured evidence collection, and taxonomy based classification. Through controlled prompt ablations and large scale deployment, we conduct structured failure analysis across the ReAct loop (observation, reasoning, and action), identifying key limitations of LLM agents during dark pattern audits.
  • Turning Trust to Transactions: Tracking Affiliate Marketing and FTC Compliance in YouTube’s Influencer
    We develop a scalable measurement pipeline that combines web measurement and NLP to detect affiliate marketing and disclosure compliance across 2 million YouTube videos. Our study reveals widespread use of affiliate links alongside persistently low FTC-compliant disclosures, and shows that platform-level interventions are most strongly associated with improved transparency in the influencer economy.
  • Information about Data
    We investigate a critical but overlooked assumption in privacy regulation, namely that firms understand their own data practices, by modeling corporate knowledge and empirically studying internal data governance across organizations. Through a mixed-methods study combining interviews and surveys, we identify organizational barriers such as information silos and propose disclosure-based mechanisms to improve internal awareness and regulatory effectiveness.
  • Faults and Pitfalls in Implementing the Right to be Forgotten
    We present a two-phase framework for implementing RTBF that integrates legal reasoning with enforcement-driven system design, and evaluate it through a case study on Elasticsearch. Our analysis of real-world enforcement cases reveals recurring failure modes and system-level anti-patterns, highlighting key tradeoffs in achieving compliant and efficient data deletion.
  • GDPRxiv: Establishing the State of the Art in GDPR Enforcement
    We build GDPRxiv, a large-scale automated system that collects and curates enforcement decisions, legal precedents, and regulatory guidance to systematically characterize how GDPR is enforced in practice. By aggregating 8,000+ official documents across jurisdictions, our work enables data-driven analysis of enforcement trends and reveals common compliance failures in real-world systems.
